Doxorubicin (DOX) Profile

Mechanism: DNA intercalation, topoisomerase II inhibition, ROS generation (Carvalho et al., 2009).

Side Effects:

  • Most Common and Critical:
    • Cardiotoxicity (Heart Damage): Cardiomyopathy, arrhythmias, heart failure (Lipshultz et al., 1995).
    • Hematological: Neutropenia, anemia, thrombocytopenia (MacDonald, 2009).
    • Gastrointestinal: Nausea, vomiting, mucositis, diarrhea (Griffin et al., 1996).
  • Other Common:
    • Hair Loss (Alopecia)
    • Urine Discoloration
    • Fatigue
    • Chemo Brain (cognitive issues)
    • Injection Site Reactions
    • Nail and Skin Changes
  • Less Common:
    • Cough or hoarseness with fever or chills
    • Darkening or redness of skin (post-radiation)
    • Fast/irregular heartbeat
    • Fever or chills
    • Joint pain
    • Lower back/side pain with fever
    • Pain at injection site
    • Painful/difficult urination with fever
    • Red streaks along injected vein
    • Shortness of breath
    • Stomach pain
    • Swelling of feet/lower legs
